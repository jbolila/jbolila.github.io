Identity "Sign in with your email"

A few days ago Adobe lost 150 million customer passwords and got attention for the well known experts on computers security and cryptographers, like Bruce Schneier on his blog and Steve Gibbson on his Twitter:

And from the experts is also possible to know the top passwords . As expected and in any other site the most common passwords are: 123456, 123456789, password, 12345678, qwerty, 1234567, 111111, 123123.

Isn't easy or cheap contract one cryptographer or security experts like those.

For me as a programmer interested in this themes it is almost impossible maintain one solution of password storage and validation, that protects my reputation and the regular customer during the life time of a service (one good solution today can be less effective tomorrow). And I don't have all of my time dedicated to development and maintenance of authentication systems.

Pro Tip: The message, “Password contains invalid characters” means your security team, architects and IT people need to be fired. — Nick Selby ‏@nselby

One alternative is to delegate this functionality for one well known and trusted entity and from here we have a decision to make.

Or follow the current leaders of OAuth, Facebook, Google, Twitter and Yahoo and obligate our customers to have one account in one of this social services, (worst some sites have a shortened list and give just two options to facilitate the decision and just allow Facebook or Google) and obligate the customer to give up is own privacy.

Or in alternative we can ask our customers for something they have most, 2.2 billion, his email address and nothing more.

Mozilla Persona is the service for authenticate users over the Internet based on email address, keeping the privacy (the only information you got from Mozilla is the email address of the authenticated user).

“Simple, privacy-sensitive single sign-in: let your users sign into your website with their email address, and free yourself from password management.”

For this and expressing myself in Perl I have developed the Plack::Middleware::Auth::BrowserID module. This allows any Plack solution to provide functionality of login and logout, in a very simple way.
